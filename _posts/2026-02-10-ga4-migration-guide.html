Why GA4 migration matters now

UA is sunset, and AI-driven reporting depends on clean, consistent data. If your GA4 setup is messy, every automated insight and bidding model will optimize the wrong signal.

The shift: sessions to events

GA4 is event-first. To make it AI-friendly, events must be consistent and meaningful.

  • Core events: page_view, form_submit, generate_lead, purchase
  • Context parameters: page_title, page_location, button_text, value
  • User properties: customer_type, plan_tier, lifecycle_stage

Step 1: design the measurement plan

I start by mapping the funnel and defining a small set of business outcomes. This avoids the common track everything mess that kills reporting quality.

Step 2: build a clean data layer

Structured data layers make GA4 reliable and reusable. They also make it easier for AI tools to interpret intent and outcomes.

Step 3: implement with GTM

GTM lets you deploy events without code changes and gives you a consistent QA workflow. If you use server-side tagging, you also improve attribution reliability.

Step 4: choose conversions carefully

Pick 3 to 5 conversions that actually represent revenue or pipeline. AI bidding depends on clean conversion data, so this is non-negotiable.

Step 5: connect GA4 to your AI stack

  • Enable BigQuery export for raw data access
  • Sync GA4 with Ads, Search Console, and CRM
  • Standardize UTM naming for model accuracy

AI-era reporting checklist

  • Event names are human-readable
  • Parameters are consistent across pages
  • Consent mode is configured correctly
  • Internal traffic is filtered
